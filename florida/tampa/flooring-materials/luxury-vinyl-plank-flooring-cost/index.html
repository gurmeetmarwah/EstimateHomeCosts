Waterproof Performance

Luxury vinyl plank flooring is one of the most popular waterproof flooring options for modern homes.

Best areas for waterproof LVP:

  • Kitchens — spills and splashes near sinks and appliances
  • Bathrooms — rigid core with sealed transitions (check product rating)
  • Basements — slab moisture when proper barrier is used
  • Laundry rooms — washer leaks and humidity

LVP Installation Types

Floating floor

Difficulty: Moderate · Labor: $$

Planks click together over underlayment — no glue to subfloor. Most DIY-friendly rigid-core installs.

Glue-down

Difficulty: Higher · Labor: $$$

Permanent bond — best for commercial and high-traffic areas. Harder removal later.

Click-lock

Difficulty: Easy–moderate · Labor: $$

Most popular residential method. Good durability when expansion gaps are respected.

Loose lay

Difficulty: Easy · Labor: $

Heavy planks with friction backing — faster install, check manufacturer specs for traffic rating.

Luxury Vinyl Plank FAQ

How much does luxury vinyl plank flooring cost?

Luxury vinyl plank typically costs $4–$12 per square foot installed nationally, or $2,500–$12,000 for 500–1,000 sq ft projects. Use the estimator above for your style and city.

Is LVP waterproof?

Most SPC and WPC rigid-core LVP is waterproof when seams and perimeters are sealed correctly. Always confirm the product rating for baths and basements.

Does LVP increase home value?

LVP improves perceived condition vs old carpet or damaged floors, but solid hardwood still commands higher resale premiums in many markets.

Is SPC better than WPC?

SPC is harder and more dent-resistant. WPC feels slightly softer and warmer underfoot. Both are excellent waterproof options.

How long does LVP last?

With a 12–20 mil wear layer, expect 15–25 years in typical homes; commercial-grade mil ratings can go longer.
