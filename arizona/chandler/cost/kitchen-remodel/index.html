Kitchen Remodel Cost by Component

How each line item contributes to your total — click to expand national installed ranges

Cabinets

Cabinets are typically the largest single expense — 30–40% of a full remodel. Stock lines run $4,000–$12,000 installed; semi-custom $12,000–$25,000; custom $25,000–$50,000+. Include demolition, fillers, crown, and hardware in your budget.

Countertops

Countertops account for 10–15% of total cost. Laminate: $1,500–$4,000; quartz: $3,500–$8,500; granite: $3,800–$9,500; marble: $5,500–$14,000+ for a medium kitchen with island.

Flooring

New kitchen flooring runs $2,500–$8,000 installed depending on material and subfloor prep. Luxury vinyl plank and porcelain tile are the most popular choices in 2026; hardwood adds cost when matching existing adjacent rooms.

Appliances

A full appliance package (refrigerator, range, dishwasher, microwave) costs $4,000–$12,000 at mid-range and $15,000–$35,000+ for pro-style brands. Panel-ready units add cabinetry coordination labor.

Backsplash

Tile backsplash installation runs $800–$3,500 for a typical run behind the range and counters. Slab backsplash matching countertops can add $2,000–$6,000.

Lighting

Kitchen lighting upgrades budget $1,200–$4,500 for recessed cans, pendants, and under-cabinet LED. Smart dimming and additional circuits may require electrical panel capacity review.

Plumbing

Plumbing rough-in and finish for a standard remodel runs $1,800–$5,500. Moving the sink or adding a pot filler adds $1,500–$4,000+ per relocation.

Island

A new kitchen island with cabinets, countertop, and electrical costs $3,500–$12,000+ installed. Structural floor reinforcement or gas cooktop rough-in increases the total.

Sink & faucet

Undermount sink and quality faucet packages run $400–$1,800 for mid-range; workstation sinks and touchless faucets reach $2,000–$4,500 installed with plumbing labor.

Layout Changes & Plumbing Costs

Moving fixtures is the fastest way to increase labor and permit scope. Plan the work triangle before committing to a new layout.

  • Move kitchen sink: $1,500 – $4,500 (drain + supply reroute)
  • Relocate dishwasher: $800 – $2,200
  • Move gas range / cooktop: $2,000 – $6,500+ (new gas line, vent hood)
  • Remove non-load-bearing wall: $1,200 – $4,000
  • Remove load-bearing wall + beam: $4,000 – $12,000+
  • Add pot filler or second sink: $600 – $2,500

Keeping the sink, range, and refrigerator in their current locations typically saves 15–25% on total labor versus a full layout reconfiguration.

When a layout change is worth it

  • Opening to a living area improves flow and resale appeal
  • Adding an island requires clearances (42" walkways minimum)
  • Gas line extensions need licensed plumbers and inspections
  • Verify load-bearing status before any wall demo

Kitchen Remodel Permit Costs

Permits protect your investment and are required for most work beyond cosmetic surface updates. Your contractor typically pulls permits before rough-in inspections.

  • Building / remodel permit: $200 – $600
  • Electrical permit: $80 – $250
  • Plumbing permit: $80 – $300
  • Gas line permit: $100 – $350

Total permit fees for a full kitchen remodel typically run $500–$1,200 nationally. HOA review may add time but not always cost.

Work that usually requires permits

  • Moving or adding plumbing drains and supplies
  • New or relocated gas lines for ranges and cooktops
  • Electrical panel upgrades or new circuits
  • Removing or modifying load-bearing walls
  • Structural changes for a new island or pass-through

Kitchen Remodel ROI

Kitchens are the most scrutinized room at resale. A thoughtful mid-range update typically returns 50–70% of project cost nationally, with variation by market and scope.

  • Minor cosmetic refresh: 60–75% recovery — lower cost, faster sale
  • Mid-range full remodel: 50–65% recovery — sweet spot for most homes
  • Luxury over-improvement: 40–55% recovery — may not match neighborhood comps

Focus on layout, clean surfaces, and functional storage. Buyers notice dated cabinets and worn counters more than pro-style ranges in average-priced neighborhoods.

Best ROI upgrades in 2026

  • Quartz or quality stone countertops
  • Semi-custom or refaced cabinets in neutral tones
  • LED lighting and a functional backsplash
  • Stainless or black stainless appliance package
  • Improved work triangle without over-customizing

Common Kitchen Remodel Mistakes

Avoid these costly errors that blow budgets and timelines

Budget & planning

  • Skipping a 15–20% contingency for hidden issues behind walls
  • Ordering cabinets before final measurements are verified
  • Not checking appliance dimensions against cabinet openings
  • Underestimating permit and inspection lead times

Design & execution

  • Insufficient counter space on both sides of the range
  • Poor lighting — relying on a single ceiling fixture only
  • Island too large for clear walkway clearances
  • Hiring unlicensed trades for gas, plumbing, and electrical

Kitchen Remodel FAQs

Answers to the most-searched questions about kitchen remodel cost in 2026

How much does a kitchen remodel cost in 2026?

The national average kitchen remodel cost in 2026 is $18,000–$75,000 for a full kitchen, with most homeowners paying around $42,500 for a mid-range update. Cosmetic refreshes run $10,000–$25,000; luxury kitchens often reach $60,000–$150,000+ with custom cabinetry and premium appliances.

How long does a kitchen remodel take?

A typical full kitchen remodel takes 6–12 weeks from demolition to final punch list. Layout changes, custom cabinets, or supply delays can extend the timeline to 14–16 weeks. Cosmetic updates with stock cabinets may finish in 4–6 weeks.

What is the ROI on a kitchen remodel?

Homeowners typically recover 50–70% of a mid-range kitchen remodel cost at resale, according to national remodeling reports. Minor cosmetic updates often return a higher percentage than over-the-top luxury kitchens relative to neighborhood norms.

Do I need a permit for a kitchen remodel?

Yes, most U.S. cities require permits when you alter plumbing, gas lines, electrical circuits, or load-bearing walls. Permit fees for kitchen work typically run $500–$1,200 nationally. Licensed contractors usually pull permits for rough-in plumbing, gas, and electrical inspections.

How much do kitchen cabinets cost?

Stock cabinets cost $4,000–$12,000 installed for a typical kitchen. Semi-custom runs $12,000–$25,000; fully custom cabinetry ranges from $25,000–$50,000+ depending on wood species, inserts, and finish level.

Is it worth moving the kitchen sink or gas range?

Moving the sink, dishwasher, or gas range adds $3,000–$12,000+ to your project because it requires new rough-in plumbing or gas lines and often opening floors or walls. It is worth it when the new layout significantly improves the work triangle or opens the room — otherwise, keeping fixtures in place saves 15–25% on labor.

What kitchen upgrades add the most value?

Quality countertops, updated cabinets, modern lighting, and a functional layout deliver strong buyer appeal. Stainless or panel-ready appliances and a tile backsplash are high-impact mid-range choices. Avoid over-improving beyond what comparable homes in your area support.
