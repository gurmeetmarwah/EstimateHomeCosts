      Roof Replacement Labor Costs

      Labor typically accounts for 50–60% of your total roof replacement bill. Rates depend on crew experience, roof complexity, pitch, and local demand.

      • National average: $2.45–$4.50 per sq ft for installation
      • Steep-slope premium: +15–35% for pitches above 8/12
      • Multi-story homes: +7–14% for safety equipment and access
      • Complex layouts: +$500–$2,000 for dormers, valleys, skylights

      Permits & Insurance for Roof Replacement

      Nearly all municipalities require a permit for full roof replacement. Insurance may cover storm damage — but not normal wear.

      • Typical permit fee: $150–$500 for residential re-roof
      • Inspection fees: $50–$150 (often bundled with permit)
      • Insurance claims: Hail, wind, fallen trees — minus deductible
      • Depreciation: Older roofs may see reduced payout

      Always verify your contractor pulls the correct permit. Unpermitted work can void claims and complicate home sales.

      Roof Cost Calculator FAQs

      Answers to the most-searched questions about roof replacement pricing

      How much does a new roof cost?

      The national average roof replacement cost is $8,000–$24,000 for a typical home, or roughly $3.50–$9.50 per square foot installed. Most homeowners pay around $14,500 for architectural asphalt shingles on a 2,000 sq ft roof including tear-off and disposal.

      How accurate is a roof cost calculator?

      Online calculators provide a useful budget range within about 10–15% of final bids when you enter accurate roof size, material, and pitch. Decking repairs, code upgrades, and contractor overhead can push the final number higher — always get 3–5 on-site estimates.

      What is the cheapest roofing material?

      Architectural asphalt shingles are the most affordable full replacement option at roughly $4.75–$8.00 per sq ft installed nationally. Metal and tile cost more upfront but often deliver lower cost per year over their lifespan. See our metal vs. shingles comparison.

      Does roof pitch affect replacement cost?

      Yes. Steep roofs require more safety equipment and slower production — typically adding 15–35% over a walkable low-slope roof. Use the pitch options in our advanced calculator to see the impact on your estimate.

      Is tear-off included in roof replacement quotes?

      Most full replacement quotes include tear-off and disposal at $0.75–$1.25 per sq ft. You can toggle tear-off in the calculator to compare overlay savings where local code allows a single additional layer.

      How many roofing squares is my roof?

      One roofing square equals 100 square feet of roof area. A 2,000 sq ft roof is 20 squares. The advanced calculator shows squares as you adjust the size slider.
