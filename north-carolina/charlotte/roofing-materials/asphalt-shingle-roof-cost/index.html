Asphalt shingle styles

Three main tiers — each with different cost, curb appeal, and lifespan.

3-tab shingles

  • Cost: $3.50–$5.50 / sq ft
  • Lifespan: 15–20 years
  • Curb appeal: Basic
Pros
  • Lowest upfront cost of any asphalt style
  • Fastest install — often 1–2 days on typical homes
  • Easy to patch individual tabs after wind damage
  • Available from every major roofing brand
Cons
  • Shortest lifespan — may need replacement in 15 years
  • Flat, uniform look — less dimensional curb appeal
  • Lower wind ratings than architectural lines
  • More prone to blow-off on exposed edges

Architectural shingles

  • Cost: $4.75–$8.00 / sq ft
  • Lifespan: 25–30 years
  • Curb appeal: High
Pros
  • Best value — stronger warranty per dollar spent
  • Dimensional profile mimics wood shake or slate
  • 130+ mph wind ratings on most premium lines
  • Top choice for resale and suburban neighborhoods
Cons
  • 15–25% higher cost than 3-tab on same roof
  • Heavier — may require deck inspection before install
  • Still shorter-lived than metal, tile, or slate
  • Algae streaks possible without AR-coated granules

Luxury shingles

  • Cost: $5.50–$9.00 / sq ft
  • Lifespan: 30–40 years
  • Curb appeal: Premium
Pros
  • Thickest profile — slate or shake appearance
  • Longest asphalt lifespan with proper ventilation
  • Class 4 impact options for hail-prone markets
  • Strongest curb appeal in the asphalt category
Cons
  • Highest asphalt price — nears entry-level metal cost
  • Fewer installers certified on designer product lines
  • Extra weight — structural load must be verified
  • Longer lead times for specialty colors and styles

      Asphalt Shingle FAQ

      How long do asphalt shingles last?

      3-tab shingles last 15–20 years. Architectural shingles last 25–30 years. Luxury designer lines can reach 30–40 years with proper attic ventilation and maintenance.

      Are architectural shingles worth it?

      Yes for most homes. They cost 15–25% more than 3-tab but add thickness, wind resistance, longer warranties, and better curb appeal — the default choice for resale-focused re-roofs.

      Do asphalt shingles increase home value?

      A new architectural shingle roof helps pass inspections and signals maintenance to buyers. Typical resale recovery is 50–60% of project cost in suburban markets.

      Which asphalt shingles are best?

      Look for a minimum 130 mph wind rating, algae-resistant granules in humid climates, and Class 4 impact rating in hail zones. Major brands include GAF, Owens Corning, CertainTeed, and Malarkey.
