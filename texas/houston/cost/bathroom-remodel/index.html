Bathroom Lighting Costs

Good lighting is one of the highest-ROI upgrades in a modern bath. Plan layers: ambient, task, and accent.

  • Vanity sconces (pair): $150 – $600 installed
  • Recessed cans (per light): $175 – $350 each
  • LED mirror / medicine cabinet: $400 – $1,800
  • Shower waterproof can: $250 – $450
  • Dimmer switches & wiring: $200 – $600

Most remodels budget $800–$2,500 for lighting upgrades when replacing fixtures and adding cans. High-end smart lighting systems can exceed $4,000.

Lighting tips that save money

  • Place sconces at eye level beside the mirror — not overhead only
  • Use damp-rated fixtures in shower zones (IP44 or higher)
  • LED retrofits cut long-term energy use vs. incandescent
  • Combine switched zones so you do not need all lights on at once

Bathroom Remodel Labor Costs

Labor typically accounts for 45–55% of your total bathroom remodel bill. Licensed plumbers and electricians are required for most rough-in work.

  • General contractor coordination: 15–20% of project cost
  • Plumbing rough-in & finish: $2,500 – $8,000+
  • Tile installation: $8 – $18 per sq ft labor
  • Layout change premium: +$2,500 – $8,000 for moving drains

Common Bathroom Remodel Mistakes

Avoid these costly errors that blow budgets and timelines

Budget & planning

  • Skipping a 15–20% contingency for hidden water damage
  • Choosing materials before confirming lead times
  • Not verifying vent fan ducting to exterior
  • Underestimating permit and inspection delays

Design & execution

  • Insufficient shower waterproofing (flood test skipped)
  • Poor vanity lighting — shadows on the face
  • Tile layout not dry-fitted — awkward cuts at entry
  • Hiring unlicensed trades for plumbing and electrical

Bathroom Remodel FAQs

Answers to the most-searched questions about modern bathroom remodel cost in 2026

How much does a modern bathroom remodel cost in 2026?

The national average modern bathroom remodel cost in 2026 is $15,000–$45,000 for a full bath, with most homeowners paying around $31,800 for a mid-range update. Half baths run $15,000–$25,000; master baths often reach $40,000–$90,000+ for luxury finishes.

How long does a bathroom remodel take?

A typical full bathroom remodel takes 3–6 weeks from demolition to final fixtures. Layout changes, custom tile, or supply delays can extend the timeline to 8–10 weeks. Half-bath refreshes may finish in 2–3 weeks.

What is the ROI on a bathroom remodel?

Homeowners typically recover 60–70% of a mid-range bathroom remodel cost at resale, according to national remodeling reports. Master baths and spa-style upgrades appeal to buyers but have lower percentage returns than simpler updates in secondary baths.

Do I need a permit for a bathroom remodel?

Yes, most U.S. cities require permits when you alter plumbing, electrical, or structural walls. Permit fees for bathroom work typically run $350–$800 nationally. Licensed contractors usually pull permits for rough-in plumbing and electrical inspections.

How much does a frameless glass shower cost?

A frameless glass shower enclosure costs $2,800–$6,500 installed nationally, depending on panel size, hardware finish, and door type. Framed units cost less ($1,200–$2,800); tub-shower combos are the most budget-friendly option at $800–$2,200 for the surround.

Is it worth moving bathroom plumbing for a new layout?

Moving the toilet, shower, or vanity drain adds $2,500–$8,000+ to your project because it requires opening floors or walls and new rough-in plumbing. It is worth it when the new layout significantly improves flow or adds a double vanity — otherwise, keeping fixtures in place saves 15–30% on labor.

What bathroom features add the most value?

Double vanities, walk-in showers with frameless glass, quality tile, and good lighting deliver strong buyer appeal. Heated floors and smart mirrors are popular upgrades with moderate cost. Avoid over-improving a secondary bath beyond neighborhood norms.
