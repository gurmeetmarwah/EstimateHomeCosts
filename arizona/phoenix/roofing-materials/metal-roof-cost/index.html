Metal roofing systems

Three common installs — standing seam is the premium default for residential re-roofs.

Standing seam

  • Cost: $10–$16 / sq ft
  • Lifespan: 40–70 years
  • Curb appeal: Premium modern
Pros
  • Concealed fasteners — clean lines, fewer leak points
  • Best wind and water performance among metal types
  • PVDF / Kynar coatings for long color retention
  • Strong resale in hail and fire-prone markets
Cons
  • Highest metal upfront cost
  • Requires experienced standing seam crews
  • Expansion clips and detailing add labor
  • Not ideal for very low-slope roofs

Corrugated / exposed fastener

  • Cost: $9–$13 / sq ft
  • Lifespan: 30–45 years
  • Curb appeal: Rustic / utilitarian
Pros
  • Lower material and labor than standing seam
  • Fast install on barns, shops, and outbuildings
  • Good snow shedding on steep pitches
  • Wide gauge and profile options
Cons
  • Exposed fasteners need periodic inspection
  • Less refined look for upscale suburban homes
  • More oil-canning risk on long panels
  • Weaker aesthetic match for HOA neighborhoods

Metal shingles

  • Cost: $9.50–$14 / sq ft
  • Lifespan: 40–60 years
  • Curb appeal: Traditional profile
Pros
  • Mimics shake or slate without the weight
  • Concealed or low-profile fastening on many lines
  • HOA-friendly option where bare panels are restricted
  • Metal durability with familiar shingle silhouette
Cons
  • More pieces and trim than standing seam
  • Premium metal shingle lines approach seam pricing
  • Color match across batches matters on repairs
  • Fewer installers than asphalt in some markets

      Phoenix, AZ — Local Cost Context

      Prices on this page adjust for Phoenix-area labor, permits, and climate — not a generic national template.

      Labor

      Phoenix metro labor is shaped by extreme summer heat — roofers start at dawn and HVAC crews book months ahead before peak season. Maricopa County's growth keeps new-construction and retrofit crews in steady demand.

      Contractor labor runs about 5% above the U.S. average; typical permit fees in our model start around $248 for standard residential work.

      Permits

      City of Phoenix and Maricopa County cities (Mesa, Chandler, Gilbert) use online permitting for most residential work. Solar requires utility interconnection with APS or SRP.

      Climate

      Sonoran Desert extremes drive every Phoenix-area project decision:

      • 120°F+ days increase cooling load and shorten shingle lifespan
      • UV degradation affects roofing, paint, and fence materials within 10–15 years
      • Monsoon wind and dust storms test roof fastening and drainage

      Top local projects

      • Tile or cool-roof replacement — Heat resistance and longevity justify upfront cost
      • High-SEER central AC — Essential before summer peak — book early
      • Rooftop solar system — Among the best production-per-square-foot markets in the U.S.

      Metal Roof FAQ

      How long does a metal roof last?

      Standing seam systems often last 40–70 years with quality PVDF coatings. Corrugated panels may fall in the 30–45 year range depending on gauge, coating, and environment.

      Is a metal roof worth the extra cost?

      If you plan to stay 15+ years, metal frequently delivers a lower cost per year than asphalt despite higher upfront price. Hail resistance, fire ratings, and energy savings add value in many regions.

      Are metal roofs noisy in rain?

      Over solid decking with underlayment and attic insulation, rain noise is typically comparable to shingles. Noise issues usually trace to open-frame barn installs without full assemblies.

      Standing seam vs corrugated — which should I choose?

      Standing seam is the premium residential choice: concealed fasteners, cleaner lines, and better long-term performance. Corrugated costs less and suits outbuildings or rustic aesthetics.
