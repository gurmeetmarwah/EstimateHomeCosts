HVAC Installation Labor Costs

HVAC labor costs nationally typically range from $75–$150 per hour. Installation labor is usually 45–55% of your total project cost.

  • Standard replacement: 8–14 labor hours ($600–$2,100)
  • New ductwork: +16–32 hours depending on home layout
  • Complex installs: multi-zone, attic units, or crane lifts add 15–25%
  • Peak season (June–August): demand can push rates 10–15% higher

Energy Efficiency, SEER Ratings & Rebates

Higher SEER ratings cost more upfront but cut summer electric bills — critical in warm climates where AC runs much of the year.

SEER ratings explained

  • 14 SEER (standard): Minimum in many states; lowest install cost
  • 16 SEER (high): ~12% more efficient than 14 SEER; best value in most U.S. climates
  • 18+ SEER (premium): Up to 25% savings vs. 14 SEER; ideal for large homes

Estimated annual savings (2,000 sq ft)

  • 14 → 16 SEER: $180 – $280/year off cooling
  • 14 → 18 SEER: $320 – $450/year
  • Payback on upgrade premium: typically 4–7 years

2026 rebates & tax credits

  • Federal tax credit: up to $2,000 for qualifying heat pumps
  • Utility rebates (varies by provider): $200 – $1,200 for high-efficiency equipment
  • Ask your contractor — many handle rebate paperwork

HVAC Repair vs. Replacement

Know when a repair makes sense and when a new system saves money on energy and breakdowns.

HVAC repair

$150 – $800

per service call (parts extra)

  • System under 10 years old with a single failed component
  • Repair cost under 30% of replacement price
  • Refrigerant leak, capacitor, thermostat, or blower issues
  • Still meeting cooling/heating demand efficiently

Repair when: the unit is young and the fix is under $1,500.

Full replacement

$4,500 – $18,000

typical national range

  • Unit is 12–15+ years old (AC) or 15–20+ (furnace)
  • R-22 refrigerant system — expensive to recharge
  • Rising energy bills or uneven cooling rooms
  • Repairs exceed $3,000 or happen twice in 12 months

Replace when: repair quote exceeds 50% of a new system or energy bills jumped 25%+.

Local Climate & HVAC Considerations

Climate drives system sizing, efficiency priorities, and long-term operating costs.

Hot & dry climates

  • Extreme summer heat — oversizing risks short-cycling and humidity issues
  • High cooling load; prioritize SEER and proper duct sealing
  • Heat pumps work well in mild climates; reduced heating demand in winter
  • Attic installations need extra insulation around ductwork

Florida

  • High humidity — variable-speed systems and dehumidification matter
  • Coastal corrosion requires coated coils and regular maintenance
  • Mold prevention depends on proper duct insulation

Arizona

  • Extreme dry heat — very high cooling load per sq ft
  • Solar + high-SEER AC pairs well for peak summer bills
  • Minimal heating needs; heat pumps still viable

HVAC Permits & Inspections

Nearly all municipalities require a permit for HVAC installation. Permit fees typically run $150–$500 for residential work, and your licensed contractor usually pulls the permit.

  • Mechanical permit: $150–$350 for residential HVAC change-out
  • Inspection: Rough-in and final — usually included in permit fee
  • Timeline: Same-day to 3 business days for online filing
  • Contractor responsibility: Licensed HVAC pros pull permits on your behalf

HVAC Installation FAQs

Answers optimized for voice search, AI overviews, and People Also Ask.

How much does HVAC installation cost?

HVAC installation typically costs $4,500–$18,000 nationally for a typical 2,000 sq ft home. Most homeowners pay around $8,200 for central AC at 16 SEER, depending on ductwork and efficiency. Use our calculator for a personalized range.

Does HVAC replacement include ductwork?

Most quotes assume existing ductwork in good condition. New duct installation adds $2,800–$6,500. Always inspect ducts for leaks — sealing costs far less than full replacement.

What size HVAC system do I need?

A typical 2,000 sq ft home usually needs a 3.5–4 ton unit, but Manual J load calculation is the only accurate method. Oversized systems short-cycle; undersized units run constantly during peak summer heat.

Is a heat pump cheaper than AC?

Heat pumps cost 10–20% more than AC-only but replace both furnace and air conditioner. In many regions, a heat pump often wins on total equipment cost versus separate systems.

How long does HVAC installation take?

Standard replacements take 1–2 days. New ductwork or multi-zone mini-splits take 3–5 days. Permit approval in most cities may add 1–3 business days before work starts.

What SEER rating should I choose?

16 SEER is the sweet spot for most U.S. homes — meaningful savings over 14 SEER without the premium of 18+. High-usage homes benefit most from 18+ SEER and utility rebates.
