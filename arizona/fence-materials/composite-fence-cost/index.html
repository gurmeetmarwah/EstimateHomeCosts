Composite Fence Pros & Cons

Advantages

  • Wood appearance without annual staining
  • Resists rot, insects, and splitting
  • Long warranty on premium lines
  • Strong HOA and resale appeal

Disadvantages

  • Highest upfront cost among common materials
  • Can fade slightly over many years in harsh sun
  • Not all contractors stock every brand

Why Homeowners Choose Composite

Composite bridges the gap between wood aesthetics and vinyl maintenance.

  • Curb appeal — realistic grain and multi-tone boards
  • Durability — no warped pickets or rotting posts
  • Privacy — solid 6 ft systems common
  • Value — lower lifetime cost than repeatedly replacing wood

    Composite Fence FAQ

    How much does a composite fence cost?

    Composite fencing typically costs $32–$52 per linear foot installed. Use the calculator above.

    Is composite better than wood?

    Composite costs more upfront but needs far less maintenance — often better lifetime value.

    How long does composite fencing last?

    Most systems are rated 25–30 years with minimal upkeep.
