Vinyl vs Wood Fence (2026): Cost, Pros & Which to Choose

Vinyl and wood are the two most popular privacy fence materials in the U.S. For 150 linear feet of 6 ft fencing with one gate, wood runs $3,750–$5,550 installed nationally; comparable vinyl runs $4,200–$6,150. Vinyl costs more upfront but typically wins on lifetime maintenance; wood wins on natural look and lower initial price.

Quick verdict

Choose wood for lowest upfront cost; vinyl for lowest lifetime maintenance

If you want the lowest upfront cost and enjoy maintaining a natural wood look, pressure-treated pine or cedar is the straightforward pick: national installed pricing near $25–$37 per linear ft for 6 ft privacy. Plan to stain or seal every few years and replace boards as they age.

Vinyl privacy fencing makes sense when you want zero staining, uniform color for HOA compliance, or long warranty coverage in humid climates. At $28–$41 per linear ft installed, vinyl costs more today—but skipping $200–$400 maintenance cycles every few years often closes the gap within a decade.

Vinyl vs Wood Fence Costs in 2026

Fence quotes bundle posts, panels or pickets, concrete, gates, hardware, and permits. On 150 linear feet of 6 ft privacy fencing, most homeowners see $4,800 for wood and $5,400 for vinyl—before slope work, removal, or premium gates.

  • Wood pickets/panels: $8 – $14 per linear ft materials
  • Vinyl fence outdoor unit: $10 – $18 per linear ft materials
  • Fence labor: $12 – $22 per linear ft installed
  • Gates (each): $350 – $900 installed
  • Permits: $50 – $200 typical residential

Cedar, board-on-board, and hog-wire accents push wood toward the top of the range. Premium vinyl colors, reinforced bottom rails, and aluminum posts add cost on the vinyl side.

Pros & Cons

Trade-offs beyond sticker price

Vinyl fence

Pros

  • Never needs paint or stain
  • HOA acceptance 20–30 year warranty on qualifying models
  • Uniform color; easy to hose clean
  • 20–30 year manufacturer warranties common
  • No splinters for kids and pets

Cons

  • Higher upfront than pressure-treated pine
  • Can look less natural than real wood
  • Extreme cold can make vinyl brittle when hit
  • Difficult to patch invisible color match

Wood fence

Pros

  • Lowest material cost for privacy look
  • Easy for DIY-friendly homeowners to repair boards
  • Natural beauty; boosts curb appeal
  • Customizable with stains and cap styles
  • Strong in historic and rustic neighborhoods

Cons

  • Annual maintenance in most climates
  • Shorter life than vinyl in wet climates
  • Posts and boards can twist or crack
  • Termites and rot in humid regions

Which Should You Choose?

Match the material to your HOA, climate, and maintenance appetite

Tight budget, willing to stain

Pressure-treated wood delivers classic privacy at the lowest installed price. Plan to stain or seal within the first year and every 2–4 years after.

HOA requires uniform white fence

Vinyl's consistent color and picket spacing satisfy most HOA design rules without ongoing staining.

Humid or rainy climate

Vinyl won't absorb moisture and rot. Wood needs pressure-treated posts and drainage away from the soil line.

Front-yard showpiece in traditional neighborhood

Cedar or redwood offers premium curb appeal. Budget for maintenance or consider composite wood alternatives.

Vinyl vs Wood Fence FAQ

Common questions from homeowners comparing fence materials in 2026

Is vinyl fencing worth it over wood?

Vinyl costs 10–25% more upfront than pressure-treated wood but needs almost no staining. It often wins on 20-year total cost in humid climates or when you want zero maintenance weekends.

How much more does vinyl fencing cost than wood in 2026?

On 150 linear feet, expect about $450–$1,200 more for vinyl versus pressure-treated wood—before premium gates or removal.

Does vinyl fence fade or crack in sun?

Quality vinyl with UV inhibitors holds color 15–25 years. Avoid bargain panels without warranty in high-sun states.

What fence height do I need for privacy?

6 feet is standard for backyard privacy. Front yards often cap at 4 feet per HOA — verify before you order.

Can vinyl fence be installed on a slope?

Panels snap into posts on many systems, but post holes and concrete are labor-intensive. Most homeowners hire pros for alignment and warranty coverage. See our fence calculator for ranges.

How long does a wood fence last vs vinyl?

Pressure-treated pine lasts 12–18 years with care; cedar 15–25 years. Vinyl often carries 20–30 year limited lifetime warranties from major manufacturers.
