Quartz vs Granite Countertops (2026): Cost, Pros & Which to Choose

Quartz and granite account for most premium U.S. kitchen remodels. For a medium kitchen (~45 sq ft) of counters with sink cutout and standard edge, quartz runs $3,500–$8,500 installed; granite runs $3,800–$9,500. Quartz wins on low maintenance; granite wins on natural stone character and heat tolerance.

Quick verdict

Choose quartz for easy care; granite for natural stone and heat resistance

For busy households and rental turnovers, engineered quartz is the practical default: non-porous surface, no sealing, and national installed pricing near $55–$95 per sq ft. If you want a wipe-clean counter with consistent pattern and strong warranty coverage, quartz almost always wins on daily upkeep.

Natural granite makes sense when you want a one-of-a-kind slab, superior heat tolerance, or a classic high-end look buyers recognize. At $60–$110 per sq ft installed, granite overlaps quartz on price but needs periodic sealing and careful cleaning on lighter stones to prevent stains.

Quartz vs Granite Countertop Costs in 2026

Countertop quotes bundle slab material, fabrication, edge profile, sink and cooktop cutouts, delivery, and installation. On a 45 sq ft L-shaped kitchen run, most homeowners see $5,500 for mid-tier quartz and $6,200 for mid-tier granite—before waterfall sides or thick mitered edges.

  • Quartz slab: $35 – $65 per sq ft (material only)
  • Granite slab: $40 – $80 per sq ft (material only)
  • Fabrication & install: $20 – $35 per sq ft typical
  • Sink cutout: $150 – $350 per cutout
  • Waterfall edge: +$800 – $2,500 per side

Level 1 granite can undercut premium quartz. Exotic granite and book-matched slabs exceed both. Always compare installed quotes—edge profile and cutouts move the total as much as brand.

Pros & Cons

Trade-offs beyond sticker price

Quartz

Pros

  • Non-porous — no sealing required
  • Consistent color and pattern lot to lot
  • Excellent stain resistance for coffee and wine
  • Manufacturer warranties often 10–25 years
  • Antimicrobial options in some lines

Cons

  • Not heat-proof — trivets required
  • Outdoor and UV exposure not recommended
  • Seams visible on long runs
  • Can discolor with harsh chemicals

Granite

Pros

  • Natural unique slab — no two alike
  • Excellent scratch and heat resistance
  • Increases perceived luxury at resale
  • Works outdoors with proper seal
  • Level 1 options can beat premium quartz on price

Cons

  • Requires periodic sealing
  • Lighter colors can stain if neglected
  • Slab variation — must approve at stone yard
  • Heavier — verify cabinet support

Which Should You Choose?

Match the countertop to your cooking habits, style, and maintenance tolerance

Busy family kitchen

Quartz handles spills without sealing. Wipe with mild soap — ideal for kids, homework, and daily cooking mess.

Serious home chef

Granite tolerates hot pans and knife work better. Use cutting boards anyway to protect both surfaces and knives.

White or marble-look aesthetic

Engineered quartz delivers bright whites and veining without marble's etching. Less upkeep than natural marble at similar visual impact.

Statement island with natural movement

Visit the stone yard and pick a granite slab with dramatic veining. Book-match seams for waterfall islands when budget allows.

Quartz vs Granite Countertops FAQ

Common questions from homeowners comparing countertop materials in 2026

Is quartz cheaper than granite?

Prices overlap. Quartz runs $55–$95/sq ft installed; granite $60–$110/sq ft. Mid-tier quartz often matches level 1–2 granite; exotic granite can cost more than premium quartz.

Which is more durable: quartz or granite?

Granite is harder and more heat-resistant. Quartz is non-porous and wins on stain resistance without sealing. Both last decades with proper care.

Does quartz look less natural than granite?

2026 quartz lines mimic marble and granite convincingly. Granite still offers unique natural movement no engineered slab fully duplicates — visit a stone yard to compare full slabs.

Can you put hot pans on quartz or granite?

Granite tolerates brief hot pot contact. Quartz can discolor from extreme heat — always use trivets on both for best results.

Which countertop is better for resale?

Both signal a premium kitchen. Quartz reads modern and low-maintenance; granite reads classic luxury. Either beats laminate for mid-range remodel ROI.

How long does countertop installation take?

Template after cabinets are set; fabrication 1–2 weeks; install typically one day. Plan plumbing disconnect for sink cutout day.
